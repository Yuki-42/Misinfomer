Chris Bongo Declares Tim Tams are 'Brainwashing Biscuits'

October 7, 2024 by Cam.S

Local conspiracy theorist Chris Bongo has once again made headlines, this time with his outrageous claim that Australia’s beloved Tim Tam biscuits are actually a sophisticated tool for mind control. Bongo, known for his unconventional views on government and corporate conspiracies, took to the streets of Sydney yesterday, handing out pamphlets and holding a homemade sign reading “Wake Up! Your Tim Tams Are Brainwashing You!”

In a livestream to his 12 dedicated YouTube followers, Bongo explained his theory in detail. “The Tim Tam isn’t just a chocolate biscuit. It’s a delivery system for subsonic frequencies that target your brain and make you more compliant. Think about it – why do you keep buying more? Why do you crave them even though you know you’ve had enough? It’s not just sugar addiction, mate, it’s mind control!”

Bongo believes that the real masterminds behind the supposed “Tim Tam Brainwashing Project” are a shadowy group he calls the Biscuit Industrial Complex, which he claims includes multinational corporations, secret government agencies, and even “elite chocolatiers” working together to keep the Australian population docile.

Bongo pointed to the biscuit’s perfect design—“two layers of chocolate-coated heaven with a creamy filling”—as evidence of the subliminal messaging he believes is embedded within. “They know exactly how to hit that sweet spot,” Bongo says. “When you bite into a Tim Tam, the pleasure centers in your brain light up, and that’s when the frequencies start. They’ve got us hooked.”

The theory took a particularly bizarre turn when Bongo announced that the “Tim Tam Slam”—a popular way of enjoying the biscuit by using it as a straw for hot drinks—was actually an initiation into deeper levels of brainwashing. According to Bongo, “The warm liquid activates the hidden neural receptors in the biscuit’s core, enhancing the transmission of the mind-control waves.”

Arnotts, the company that produces Tim Tams, responded to Bongo’s claims with a brief statement: “We can assure our customers that Tim Tams are made with love and high-quality ingredients, not mind-control devices.” Nevertheless, Bongo is undeterred, insisting that the company's response is part of a larger cover-up.

Bongo has reportedly begun stockpiling alternative biscuits, claiming that he’s “not taking any chances.” He plans to launch a nationwide “anti-Tim Tam” protest next month, encouraging Australians to reject the popular snack in favor of homemade Anzac biscuits, which he says are “brainwave neutral.”

Meanwhile, local supermarkets have reported an unexpected spike in Tim Tam sales since Bongo’s video went viral, with some customers jokingly claiming they “want to see if the mind control really works.”

Despite widespread ridicule, Chris Bongo remains convinced that his fight against the “Biscuit Industrial Complex” is just beginning. “I’m not crazy,” he said in a final message to his followers. “Once people realize they’re being controlled by their own snacks, they’ll thank me. Until then, I’ll keep fighting the good fight. Stay vigilant, Australia!”
